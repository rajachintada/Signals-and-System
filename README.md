Signals and System

UNIT–I: Introduction to Signals and Systems
Classification of signals, continuous-time and discrete-time signals, periodic and aperiodic signals, energy and power signals, elementary signals, system properties, linear and nonlinear systems, time-invariant and time-varying systems, causal and non-causal systems, stable and unstable systems.

UNIT–II: Time Domain Analysis of Continuous-Time Systems
Linear time-invariant systems, impulse response, convolution integral, properties of convolution, differential equation representation of systems, system interconnections, response of LTI systems to various inputs, impulse and step response analysis.

UNIT–III: Fourier Analysis of Signals and Systems
Fourier series representation of periodic signals, trigonometric and exponential Fourier series, Fourier transform, properties of Fourier transform, frequency response of systems, spectrum analysis, Parseval’s theorem and applications.

UNIT–IV: Laplace Transform and Continuous-Time System Analysis
Laplace transform, region of convergence, properties of Laplace transform, inverse Laplace transform, transfer function, poles and zeros, system realization, stability analysis, frequency response and system characterization using Laplace transform.

UNIT–V: Z-Transform and Discrete-Time System Analysis
Discrete-time signals and systems, difference equations, Z-transform, region of convergence, properties of Z-transform, inverse Z-transform, transfer function of discrete-time systems, stability analysis, realization of discrete-time systems, frequency response and applications.
